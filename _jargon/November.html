Boil the Ocean

In this article John Thomas distinguished engineer and director at IBM states how he would not take all of the AI tools and run them against his data banks; but rather run one at a time until his needs are fit. He states this by using the phrase “boil the ocean” to summarize how this all-or-nothing approach will turn out, “In my experience, this ‘Oh, I’m going to transform my business in one big boil-the-ocean exercise’ does not work.” I have never heard of this particular jargon before, despite it being quite popular and hated. I can see why as I had no idea what he was talking about reading out of context.

Barking Up the Wrong Tree

In this article a reader writes to a Forbes contributor with an experience they had reading one candidate's LinkedIn profile. The profile summary contained comments stating that his previous employer was run by semi-criminals , is being sued by its investors, everyone there was a scammer, and a lot more. The contributor's, Liz, reply started with the jargon, “barking up the wrong tree.” Also stating that such complaints belong on Glassdoor and not his LinkedIn profile.
